Module 2: E-Commerce Order Fulfillment – Case Study

Problem Statement

Vishalanagari’s e-commerce sector struggles with delays in order processing due to slow picking, inefficient routing, and rising delivery loads. A smart system is needed to prioritize orders, check inventory quickly, and ensure faster, optimized delivery routes.

Case Study

In Vishalanagari’s digital marketplace, online retailers face frequent delays caused by inefficient warehouse picking and suboptimal delivery routing. These inefficiencies increase operational costs and reduce customer satisfaction. A data-driven order fulfillment system is essential to ensure faster deliveries, real-time inventory checks, and optimized routing.

Algorithms Used

1. Heap Sort / Merge Sort: For prioritizing orders based on urgency, location, or time of placement.

2. Dijkstra’s Algorithm: Used for shortest-path delivery routing across the city’s dynamic road network.

3. Segment Tree: For fast inventory lookup and updates during order assignment.

Algorithm Efficiency

  • Sorting (Heap/Merge Sort): O(n log n)
  • Dijkstra’s Algorithm: O(E log V)
  • Segment Tree Queries: O(log n)
