Module 7: Digital Connectivity Optimization in Vishalanagari

Problem Statement

Vishalanagari, a bustling smart city, faces frequent network congestion in crowded public areas such as transit hubs, tourist spots, and stadiums. Connectivity drops unpredictably during peak hours, affecting both residents and visitors. The city needs a solution that dynamically manages traffic across multiple access points, ensures fair bandwidth distribution, and reallocates sessions in real-time to maintain stable, high-speed connections. This module addresses these challenges by designing an intelligent system that adapts to traffic surges, optimizes throughput, and guarantees smooth connectivity even under extreme demand.

Algorithms Used

Min-Cost Max-Flow: Routes traffic efficiently across APs to maximize throughput while minimizing latency and congestion.

Segment Tree with Lazy Propagation: Dynamically enforces per-AP bandwidth quotas during spikes, enabling fast updates over ranges.

Skip List: Tracks active sessions by priority, allowing quick reassignment when AP conditions change.

Algorithm Efficiency

Min-Cost Max-Flow: O(F * (E log V)) where F = total flow, E = edges, V = vertices.

Segment Tree (Lazy): Range updates and queries O(log n).

Skip List: Expected O(log n) for insert, delete, and search operations.
