Project Goal / Problem Statement

Vishalanagri experiences large fluctuations in daily movement — raw materials, cotton harvest trucks, office commuters, festival crowds, weather disturbances, and emergency vehicles. These unpredictable surges cause transport delays, uneven load distribution, and unstable supply chains.

During the cotton harvest season, trucks rush from farms to ginning mills. At the same time, factories demand continuous raw cotton flow. Festival traffic increases, roads choke, and even warehouses overflow. One disruption affects the entire chain — farms wait, mills slow down, factories pause production, and ports miss shipment deadlines.

Our collaboration aims to create a predictive, stable, algorithm-driven model that can sense traffic load, analyze congestion, choose alternate routes, and keep cotton & vehicle flow smooth at any hour.

Algorithms Used

• Dijkstra – Minimum-cost routing

• Bellman-Ford – Negative weights & hazard adjustments

• Floyd-Warshall – All-pairs route optimization

• Min Cost Max Flow – Smart cotton distribution across routes

Efficiency

The system reduces overall travel time, balances network load, and avoids bottlenecks by dynamically switching routes when congestion spikes. Algorithms work together to ensure faster processing, fewer delays, lower costs, and stable supply flow throughout the day.
