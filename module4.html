Module 4: Water Supply Network Optimization in Vishalanagri

Problem Statement

Vishalanagri relies on water from the Purna River to supply multiple treatment plants, residential, and industrial zones. The challenge is to design a pipeline network that distributes water efficiently with minimal cost while ensuring stable pressure during peak hours. Urban growth and varying elevation levels make the problem complex. Optimal routing, redundancy for emergencies, and cost-effective connections are critical for sustainable water management. This module focuses on using graph-based algorithms to model and optimize the water distribution network.

Algorithms Used

1. Prim’s Algorithm + Dijkstra: Prim’s algorithm is used to construct a minimum spanning tree connecting all treatment plants and zones with minimal pipeline cost. Dijkstra is applied afterward to compute the shortest paths from each water source to all zones, ensuring efficient water flow and stable pressure.

2. Kruskal’s Algorithm + Dijkstra: Kruskal provides an alternative approach to construct the minimum-cost network by sorting edges and avoiding cycles. Combining it with Dijkstra ensures shortest path water delivery from the river to all zones, verifying redundancy and efficiency in the network.

3. BFS : Breadth-first search explores the network level by level from water sources. It helps visualize reachability and identifies which zones can be supplied first during emergencies.

Efficiency of Algorithms

Prim’s Algorithm: O(E log V) – Efficient MST construction to minimize total pipeline cost.

Kruskal’s Algorithm: O(E log V) – Efficient MST alternative with cycle checking.

Dijkstra: O((V + E) log V) – Computes shortest paths for water delivery across weighted network.

BFS: O(V + E) – Traverses network level-wise to check reachability.
