Vishalnagari Candidate Selection

Problem Statement

In the smart city of Vishalnagari, local authorities and companies aim to promote local talent while ensuring that only the most qualified candidates are selected for employment.

Key characteristics of the system include:

  • Companies post monthly job openings with specific skill requirements.
  • Required skills may include programming languages, data analysis, or technical expertise.
  • The candidate pool consists of Vishalnagari residents and non-local students.

The selection rules followed by the system are:

  • Only candidates who meet all required skills are considered.
  • Vishalnagari students are given priority over non-local candidates.
  • Candidates are ranked based on experience, academic score, and certifications.

The system efficiently manages multiple job postings and a large number of candidates, ensuring fairness, prioritization, and transparency for both employers and applicants.

Algorithm Used

Heap / Priority Queue-Based Selection

  • Candidates are first filtered based on job skill requirements.
  • Eligible candidates are inserted into a max-heap.
  • Priority is calculated using:
    • Local status
    • Experience
    • Academic score
    • Certifications
  • The heap enables fast extraction of top candidates.

Algorithm Efficiency: O(n × log n) per job posting

Algorithm Flow

Design Techniques Used

  • Heap / Priority Queue: Efficient top candidate selection
  • Filtering: Ensures candidates meet skill requirements
  • Prioritization: Gives preference to Vishalnagari residents
  • Ranking: Orders candidates by merit factors
  • Decomposition: Divides the system into logical modules
  • Abstraction: Simplifies candidate data into priority scores

Relevant Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

  • SDG 4 – Quality Education: Encourages application of acquired skills
  • SDG 8 – Decent Work and Economic Growth: Promotes fair employment
  • SDG 10 – Reduced Inequalities: Balances local preference with merit
