Blood Bank Optimization System

Problem Statement

In a densely populated smart city, hospitals and emergency centers rely heavily on blood banks to save lives during surgeries, accidents, and medical emergencies. However, blood is a perishable resource with limited shelf life, and improper storage or distribution can lead to shortages during critical situations and wastage during low demand periods. Emergency requests often require immediate availability of the correct blood group, making manual coordination inefficient and error-prone. A smart blood bank optimization system is required to monitor blood inventory levels, prioritize urgent demands, and efficiently allocate blood units to hospitals in real time, ensuring maximum utilization and minimum wastage.

Algorithm Used

Greedy Algorithm combined with Priority Queue

Algorithm Efficiency: O(n log n) time due to priority queue operations and O(n) space

Algorithm Explanation

The system maintains blood unit records for different blood groups along with their expiry dates and urgency levels. Incoming blood requests from hospitals are inserted into a priority queue based on urgency and compatibility. The greedy approach ensures that the most urgent requests are fulfilled first using the closest-to-expiry blood units, thereby reducing wastage. The priority queue allows efficient selection of the highest-priority request while maintaining optimal performance even as data scales.

Design Techniques Used

Greedy Strategy: Selects optimal blood allocation at each step.
Priority Queue: Handles emergency requests efficiently.
Decomposition: Separates inventory management and request handling.
Abstraction: Represents complex medical data using priority values.
Optimization: Minimizes blood wastage and response time.
