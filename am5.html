Road Quality Monitoring & Smart Construction Analytics System

Problem Statement

The city faces continuous road damage due to heavy EV/bus usage, monsoon conditions, and poor maintenance scheduling. Repairs happen late, causing potholes, breakdowns, and traffic jams. No system exists to monitor damage in real time or prioritize which roads should be repaired first.

Solution

  • Detect cracks, bumps, and potholes using sensors/cameras
  • Track road health across EV, petrol, and goods lanes
  • Prioritize repairs based on severity, traffic load, and safety
  • Plan road construction scientifically instead of randomly

Data Structures Used

  • Graph → Represent the city road network
  • Min-Heap → Pick highest priority road repairs
  • Hash Table → Store road damage reports
  • Binary Search Tree → Sort roads by severity index

Algorithms Used

  • DFS → Detect clusters of connected damaged segments
  • BFS → Find shortest path to nearest repair team
  • Merge Sort → Sort repairs by cost/severity
  • Prim/Kruskal → Plan cost-efficient road expansion

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Sensor & mobile reports stored in Hash Table
  2. Severity Index inserted into BST
  3. Min-Heap picks high priority repairs
  4. DFS identifies clusters of road damage
  5. BFS finds nearest repair team
  6. Merge Sort sorts repairs by cost
  7. Repair team executes work & updates status

How This Innovation Supports the SDGs

SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure: Modern data-driven road repair systems improve infrastructure durability.

SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities & Communities: Smooth, well-maintained roads reduce accidents and enhance city mobility.
