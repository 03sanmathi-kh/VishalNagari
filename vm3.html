Module 1:

Problem Statement

Vishalanagari is rapidly emerging as a hub for electric vehicle (EV) production in India. The EV battery industry relies on critical raw materials like lithium, cobalt, and rare earth metals. Supply shortages, unpredictable delivery times, and fluctuating demand create bottlenecks in production. Factories often face resource wastage or delays due to inefficient allocation of these materials. Managing multiple factories simultaneously and ensuring timely battery assembly requires smart decision-making. The challenge is to dynamically distribute resources to meet demand while minimizing delays and costs. This module focuses on optimizing the flow of critical materials across the EV battery manufacturing network.

Algorithms Used

1. Greedy Allocation: Quickly assigns available materials to factories to meet immediate demand efficiently.

2. Priority Queues: Manages factories by urgency or material requirements for quick decision-making.

3. Sliding Window Forecasting: Predicts upcoming resource requirements based on recent consumption trends.

Algorithm Efficiency

  • Greedy Allocation: O(n log n) – efficient for real-time assignment of materials.
  • Priority Queues: O(log n) per operation – manages factory priority efficiently.
  • Sliding Window Forecasting: O(n) – calculates predictions over recent data with minimal overhead.
