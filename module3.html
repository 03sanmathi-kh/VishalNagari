Module 3: Urban Flood Spread Mapping in Vishalanagri

Problem Statement

Vishalanagri’s low-lying zones are highly vulnerable to floods when the Purna River overflows. Rapid urbanization and dense residential/commercial clusters make it crucial to predict how water spreads across the city. The goal is to identify high-risk areas, issue early warnings, and assist emergency teams in planning resource deployment. The simulation considers elevation, connectivity, and urban obstacles for realistic flood spread modeling.

Algorithms Used

1. Breadth-First Search (BFS): Used to simulate step-by-step flood propagation to all adjacent cells. BFS ensures that all reachable areas are visited in order of proximity to the flood source, helping to map immediate flood spread zones.

2. Dijkstra’s Algorithm: Used to simulate weighted flood spread considering elevation differences and obstacles. Helps model realistic flood arrival times to different areas, prioritizing faster flooding to lower-elevation or vulnerable zones.

3. Optional Max-Heap Simulation: Used to prioritize flood spread to the most vulnerable or critical areas first. This approach can help emergency planners focus resources on the highest-risk zones efficiently.

Efficiency of Algorithms

BFS: O(V + E) – Efficient for exploring all connected areas in the flood map without weights.

Dijkstra: O((V + E) log V) – Handles weighted flood propagation efficiently using a priority queue.

Max-Heap Simulation: O(V log V) – Efficiently prioritizes areas based on vulnerability.
