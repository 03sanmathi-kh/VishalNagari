Module 5: Real-Time Tourist Crowding Prediction in Vishalanagri

Problem Statement

Vishalanagri attracts thousands of visitors daily through its amusement parks, historic temples, museums, theme parks, film cities, and art galleries. During peak seasons, uncontrolled crowd surges lead to long queues, safety issues, traffic blocks, and reduced visitor satisfaction. The goal is to create a real-time crowd prediction system that accurately estimates footfall, detects sudden crowd spikes, and identifies the least crowded tourist spots instantly.

Algorithms Used

1. Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree): Used to process fast updates in visitor counts across tourist regions. Supports O(log n) updates and cumulative query operations.

2. Min-Heap: Used to efficiently extract the least crowded tourist attraction in real-time. Helps recommend alternate destinations to avoid overcrowding.

3. Precomputation Table: Used for predicting future peak hours using previously analyzed seasonal visitor patterns. Enables quick O(1) lookup for expected crowd density.

Efficiency of Algorithms

Fenwick Tree: O(log n) for both updates & queries

Min-Heap: O(log n) for insert & extract-min operations

Precomputation: O(1) per lookup after initial preprocessing
