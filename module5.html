Module 5: Real-Time Tourist Crowding Prediction in Vishalanagri

Problem Statement

Vishalanagri attracts thousands of visitors daily through its amusement parks, historic temples, museums, theme parks, film cities, and art galleries. During peak seasons, uncontrolled crowd surges lead to long queues, safety issues, traffic blocks, and reduced visitor satisfaction. The goal is to create a real-time crowd prediction system that accurately estimates footfall, detects sudden crowd spikes, and identifies the least crowded tourist spots instantly.

Algorithms Used

1. Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree): Efficient for updating visitor counts and querying cumulative values in O(log n).

2. Min-Heap: Quickly finds the least crowded tourist attraction to redirect visitors and reduce overcrowding.

3. Precomputation Table: Stores previously analyzed tourist patterns to instantly predict expected crowds using O(1) lookups.

Efficiency of Algorithms

Fenwick Tree: O(log n) updates, O(log n) queries

Min-Heap: O(log n) insertion & extract-min

Precomputation: O(1) querying after preprocessing

How This Problem Aligns with the SDGs?

SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth: Better crowd management ensures smoother tourism operations, resulting in higher revenue and safer working conditions.

SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities & Communities: Smart crowd prediction prevents overcrowding, protects heritage structures, and improves visitor experience.
